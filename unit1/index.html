Fire

Fire is the rapid oxidation of a material, releasing heat, light, and various reactions based in the materials burned.

Cool Fire Facts!

  1. It is possible to change the color of fire based solely on the surrounding temperature.
  2. When fire is used to burn away vegetation, the nitrogen in the soil is released into the atmosphere. The reduction in nitrogen causes the soil to lose its fertility.
    (Despite this, some plants can be used to restore the lost nitrogen, along with various other methods that have been discovered).
  3. While fire refers to the reaction, combustion, and heat caused; flame refers specifically to the seeable part of fire.

With fire being one of the basic elements, it is used in a variety of ways every day jobs and tasks.

  • Fire is used in Chemistry to raise the temperature of elements and compounds, changing their shape and reactions.
  • Fire can also be used as a lightsource with candles and fireplaces, though not as much anymore since lightbulbs were invented.
  • Fire is at the source of both smelting and forging, requiring fire to make matrials they work with malleable.
